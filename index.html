Why learn German?

More useful than
you might think.

German is the most widely spoken native language in the European Union — spoken by over 100 million people across Germany, Austria, Switzerland, Liechtenstein and parts of Belgium and Italy. It's Europe's largest economy's language, and a gateway to philosophy, music, engineering and literature that shaped the modern world.

· · · ✦ · · ·

German has a reputation for being difficult — but it's actually remarkably logical. Once you understand the patterns, the grammar makes sense in a way that English often doesn't. This app builds that understanding from day one, with clear notes on the patterns and quirks that make German, German.

FAQ

Is German really that hard to learn?
German has a reputation for difficulty, mostly because of cases and compound nouns — but the grammar is actually very logical and consistent once the patterns click. This app explains the logic clearly, with grammar tips built into every flashcard.
How does it work after I buy?
You download a single HTML file from Gumroad. Open it in any browser — Safari, Chrome, Firefox — and it runs entirely offline. No app store, no account, no internet needed after download. Your progress saves automatically in your browser.
Will my progress be saved if I close the browser?
Yes. Progress is saved to your browser's local storage automatically. It persists between sessions. You can also use the backup feature to download a save file — useful if you switch devices or browsers.
Does it work on iPhone and iPad?
Yes — it's built specifically to work on Safari for iOS. The speech features use the Web Speech API, which is well supported on modern Apple devices.
I'm a complete beginner. Is this suitable for me?
Absolutely. The Daily 10 mode is designed for beginners — it introduces 10 new words a day with clear examples and grammar tips. You can go at your own pace.
